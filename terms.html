1. The Competition

The BSA Hockey Club FIFA World Cup 2026 Score Predictor ("the Competition") is a social club sweepstake run by Bergvliet Hockey Club ("BSA") for club members and their associates. It is not open to the general public.

BSA is not affiliated with FIFA, the South African Football Association, or any official World Cup body. All match data is sourced from third-party providers.

2. Eligibility

  • Participants must be 18 years of age or older.
  • Participation is limited to BSA club members and persons to whom the competition link has been directly shared by BSA.
  • BSA committee members involved in administering the competition may participate but may not administer their own raffle draw.

3. How to Enter

  • Each match prediction costs R50.
  • To predict a match, enter your name, email address, and predicted score on the Fixtures page, then click Submit — R50.
  • You will receive a unique reference code (e.g. BSA-XXXX).
  • Pay R50 via SnapScan to the BSA World Cup 2026 campaign QR, using your reference code as the payment reference.
  • Your prediction only becomes active once payment has been confirmed. Predictions submitted without corresponding payment are void.
  • There is no limit on the number of match predictions a participant may enter.
  • Each prediction may only be submitted once per reference code. Predictions cannot be changed after submission.
  • Predictions for a given match close 30 minutes before that match's scheduled kickoff. Late submissions will not be accepted.
  • The competition closes 30 minutes before the FIFA World Cup 2026 Final.

4. Scoring & Raffle Entries

  • Exact correct score: 3 raffle entries for that prediction.
  • Correct result only (right winning team or draw, wrong score): 1 raffle entry.
  • Incorrect result: 0 raffle entries.
  • Only paid predictions are eligible for raffle entries.
  • Raffle entries accumulate across all a participant's paid predictions throughout the tournament.
  • The leaderboard ranks participants by total raffle entries earned.

5. The Prize

  • 50% of all entry fees collected (R50 × total paid entries) forms the prize pool.
  • The remaining 50% goes to BSA club funds.
  • Prize money is held by BSA and distributed after the Final.
  • The prize pool will be displayed live on the site and updated as payments are confirmed.
  • Winnings are not subject to tax in the hands of the recipient under current South African tax law for social club sweepstakes of this nature. Participants should satisfy themselves of their own tax obligations.

6. The Draw

  • The raffle draw will take place after the FIFA World Cup 2026 Final.
  • The draw is weighted: participants with more raffle entries have a proportionally higher chance of winning.
  • The draw is conducted electronically via the competition's admin panel. The process will be recorded and the result documented.
  • BSA will contact the winner using the email address provided at entry within 7 days of the Final.
  • The prize will be paid via EFT to the winner's nominated South African bank account.
  • If a winner does not respond within 14 days of being contacted, a redraw will take place.

7. Cancelled or Postponed Matches

  • If a match is officially cancelled or abandoned before completion, predictions for that match are void and the R50 entry fee will be refunded to the participant via EFT.
  • If a match is postponed but subsequently played, predictions remain valid provided they were submitted before the original scheduled kickoff time.
